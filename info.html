Paris, France

Paris, the capital of France, is known for its iconic landmarks like the Eiffel Tower and the Louvre Museum. It's a hub for art, culture, and fashion, attracting millions of visitors every year.

Quick Facts

  • Population: 2.2 million
  • Language: French
  • Currency: Euro (€)
  • Main Airport: Charles de Gaulle Airport

Top Attractions

  1. Eiffel Tower
  2. Louvre Museum
  3. Notre-Dame Cathedral
  4. Champs-Élysées
  5. Palace of Versailles

Kyoto, Japan

Kyoto is famous for its classical Buddhist temples, as well as beautiful gardens, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses. It's a place where history and nature meet in perfect harmony.

Quick Facts

  • Population: 1.5 million
  • Language: Japanese
  • Currency: Japanese Yen (¥)
  • Main Station: Kyoto Station

Top Attractions

  1. Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)
  2. Fushimi Inari Shrine
  3. Arashiyama Bamboo Grove
  4. Kiyomizu-dera Temple
  5. Nijo Castle

New York, USA

New York City, also known as "The Big Apple," is a global center for culture, art, finance, and entertainment. Landmarks like Times Square, Central Park, and the Statue of Liberty are must-see attractions.

Quick Facts

  • Population: 8.4 million
  • Language: English
  • Currency: US Dollar ($)
  • Main Airports: JFK, LaGuardia, Newark

Top Attractions

  1. Statue of Liberty
  2. Central Park
  3. Empire State Building
  4. Times Square
  5. Metropolitan Museum of Art
